Hi, I'm Archit! I am currently pursuing a research fellowship at Max Planck Institute for Informatics in Germany. Prior to that, I completed my Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering from University of Pune, India.


I wish to holistically study intelligence through various disciplines of knowledge. My long term goals are to understand what intelligence is quantitatively and qualitatively. My immediate goals are to keep on solving interesting problems which align towards my long term goals while regularly dedicating myself for some social cause. Hopefully, this greedy approach makes sense after many years. I thoroughly enjoy working on challenging problems that have a scope of impact on the research field and humanity at large. I also like working on problems that lie at the intersection of science and computing. I have designed systems, big and small, from scratch and have taken research and technical projects and frameworks from start till completion. Most of my work so far has been in the areas of Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Linguistics, Knowledge Bases, Information Retrieval and Extraction, High Performance Computing, and Automated Data Processing Pipelines.


I have had the opportunity to work with brilliant individuals in the form of mentors and colleagues. I draw inspiration from them in different ways and I am humbled to be associated with each one.


I wish to pursue higher education to dive deeper into the mysteries of the world, thus demystifying things that appear magical into smaller logical pieces that fit together in a complex way, resulting in losing the original charm of the mystery but in turn seeking the truth. As part of my immediate future plans, I intend to apply for a Master's degree for Fall 2019.

Max Planck Institute for Informatics

    Title: A Common Sense Knowledge Base Framework

    Position: Research Fellow

    Mentors:
  • Dr. Simon Razniewski
    • &
  • Prof. Dr. Gerhard Weikum


    • Ongoing efforts in the direction of building a complete framework of a commonsense knowledge base. Experiments conducted have achieved better salience and coverage than the existing knowledge bases. Such a generic framework would be domain independent and not manually engineered, but would rely on the accuracy of each stage to automatically generate and validate assertions.

Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics

    Title: Exploring Linguistic Semantic Interface with Syntactic Processing using Brain activity

    Position: Research Intern

    Collaborator:
  • Sophie Arana, PhD student


    • By conducting MEG experiments on participants reading a stimuli text corpus, the existence of a soft correlation between the P600 event related potential and the absolute difference of semantic associations was proven in order to disambiguate prepositional phrase attachments. Corpus-derived co-occurrence frequencies of stimuli words were used as a measure of semantic associations.

Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur

    Title: Automatic Extractive Text Summarizer

    Position: Research Intern

    Mentor:
  • Prof. Nisheeth Srivastava


    • A hybrid approach of using keyword frequencies (statistical approach) with automatically generated entity relationships (semantic approach) was used to combine the strengths and ameliorate the weaknesses of both the approaches, resulting into better summaries than the state-of-the-art techniques. Achieved 16.44% increase in recall and 2.17% increase in Fscore than the next best technique.

    • Collaborators:

    • A data processing pipeline was designed to run on a high performance compute cluster to synthesize images from radio interferometric data from the Giant Metrewave Radio Telescope (GMRT). Our work was presented at the 30th General Assembly of The International Astronomical Union in Vienna, the 36th Annual Meeting of The Astronomical Society of India, and is accepted at ADASS 2018.
